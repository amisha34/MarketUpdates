Nanotechnology refers to the design, manipulation, and application of materials and systems at a molecular or atomic scale. Within the medical field, this technology gives rise to Nanomedicines, which are medications developed using the principles of nanotechnology. Nanomedicines include therapies, diagnostic tools, and drug delivery systems that utilize nanoparticles, nanostructures, and nanodevices to interact with biological systems at the molecular level. These advanced drugs have revolutionized healthcare by improving the treatment of diseases and reducing side effects, providing targeted delivery, and enhancing diagnostic accuracy.



Nanomedicines are primarily used in drug delivery systems and diagnostic imaging, ensuring drugs are delivered precisely to the target area in the body. These technologies are also pivotal in cancer treatment, genetic disorders, and the development of new biosensors for quicker diagnosis and treatment planning. Nanomedicines are a significant development in personalized medicine, where treatments are tailored to individual patients' genetic profiles and specific needs.
