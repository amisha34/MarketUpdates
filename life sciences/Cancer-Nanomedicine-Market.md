Nanomedicine is a specialized field of nanotechnology that applies nanoscale materials to medical science, focusing on the diagnosis, treatment, monitoring, and control of biological systems. These nanoscale materials, often engineered as nanoparticles, enhance drug delivery mechanisms, enabling precision medicine with reduced side effects. By targeting specific cells or tissues, nanomedicine improves therapeutic outcomes and expands the horizons of medical technology. Currently, the sector is witnessing rapid innovation, with 230 nanomedicine products identified by Nanobiotix—49 of which are commercially available, while 122 are undergoing clinical trials at Phase II or III stages.
