<h2>Definition:</h2>
<p>Merkel Cell Carcinoma (MCC) is a rare and aggressive type of skin cancer that develops in the Merkel cells. It often appears as a painless, firm, shiny lump on the skin and can be challenging to diagnose accurately. MCC can metastasize quickly, making early detection and treatment crucial for patient outcomes. Effective therapeutics play a vital role in managing and treating MCC.
