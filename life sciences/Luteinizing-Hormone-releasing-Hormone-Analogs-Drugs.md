<h2>Definition:</h2>
<p>Luteinizing Hormone-releasing Hormone Analogs Drugs are synthetic hormones that mimic the activity of the natural hormone produced in the brain that plays a crucial role in regulating the production of testosterone and estrogen in the body. These drugs are commonly used in the treatment of hormone-dependent conditions such as prostate cancer and endometriosis, among others.</p>
