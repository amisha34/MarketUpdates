Microcatheters are extremely small catheters, typically ranging from 0.70 to 1.30mm in diameter, designed for various roles such as guiding wires, exchanging devices, accessing distal anatomy, crossing lesions, and delivering therapeutic embolic agents. These devices are indispensable in a wide array of diagnostic and therapeutic procedures, particularly those involving intricate blood vessel anatomy. A guidewire is an integral component of this system, as it assists in directing the catheter to the correct position inside the body.
